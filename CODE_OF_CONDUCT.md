## 🌐 1. The Operational Pledge

ZeroMomentum AI is built on the mathematical principle that **friction slows down progress**. To maintain absolute momentum in this open-source project, we pledge to keep our community environment entirely free of human friction.

We as contributors, maintainers, and engineers pledge to make participation in this architecture a harassment-free, highly collaborative experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

---

## ⚡ 2. Acceptable Telemetry (Expected Behavior)

To ensure maximum efficiency and a healthy developer environment, all active contributors must exhibit the following behaviors:

- **Constructive Debugging:** Criticize the code, not the coder. Provide exact technical reasons for why an implementation fails.
- **Empathy & Kindness:** Treat junior developers with the same respect as senior architects. We were all learning at one point.
- **Graceful Error Handling:** Accept constructive feedback gracefully. Apologize if mistakes impact the community, and implement a patch for your behavior.
- **Global Focus:** Focus on what is best for the overall ZeroMomentum AI architecture, not just your personal fork.

---

## 🛑 3. System Violations (Unacceptable Behavior)

The following actions introduce fatal friction into the community and will result in immediate termination of collaboration rights:

- ❌ **Toxicity:** Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments. Personal or political attacks.
- ❌ **Harassment:** Public or private harassment of any kind.
- ❌ **Inappropriate Imagery:** The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances.
- ❌ **Doxxing:** Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address, without explicit permission.
- ❌ **Cloud Abuse:** Intentionally spamming the live Google Cloud Run endpoints or attempting to overload the Cloud SQL database (DDoS). Respect the production resources.
- ❌ **Unprofessionalism:** Any other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in an enterprise engineering setting.

---

## ⚖️ 4. The Moderation Engine

Project maintainers are the ultimate arbiters of this Code of Conduct. We possess the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that trigger a System Violation.
